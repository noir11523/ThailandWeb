History of Thailand

Thailand's history can be broadly divided into several periods, including ancient kingdoms, the Rise of the Thai, and the modern era.

The ancient period saw the influence of Indian culture and the rise of the Khmer Empire, while the 13th century marked the emergence of independent Thai kingdoms like Sukhothai.

The Ayutthaya period, beginning in the 14th century, saw the rise of a powerful Thai kingdom, and Siam (Thailand's former name) avoided colonial domination.

Modern Thailand emerged in the late 18th century with the founding of the Chakri Dynasty, followed by reforms and modernization under kings like Mongkut and Chulalongkorn.

The 20th century saw a shift to a constitutional monarchy in 1932 and changes to the name to Thailand in 1939.
